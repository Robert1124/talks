But wait… what could go wrong?

Understanding AI’s limitations before you design your research.

AI is not deterministic

Output 1
Student appears anxious and stressed about exams.
Output 2
Student shows mild stress but remains optimistic.

What does this mean for research?

  • Results may vary from run to run.
  • Reproducibility and validity need safeguards.
  • Report model settings and run multiple trials.

AI can confidently lie

Researcher: Can you analyze the emotional patterns in this journal?
AI: Based on Bowlby’s 1989 digital attachment framework…

Hallucination detected

  • Bowlby never wrote about “digital attachment.”
  • No journal entry from March 15th.
  • “Anxious-avoidant” is not standard terminology.

Why hallucinations are dangerous

  • False patterns and incorrect inferences.
  • Fabricated citations waste time and mislead research.
  • Wrong actions in high-stakes contexts.

Student data is sacred

  • Consent: Do students understand AI analysis?
  • Storage: Where is data stored and who has access?
  • Bias: Models may perform unevenly across groups.
  • Re-identification risks and misuse.
